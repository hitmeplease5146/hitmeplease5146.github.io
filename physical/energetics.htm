ChemNotes - 3.1.4 Energetics

Enthalpy change(∆H) - Heat change under constant pressure
 
Endothermic reactions
  • Energy is absorbed
  • ∆H > 0
 Exothermic reactions
  • Energy is released
  • ∆H < 0

Standard conditions

  • 100kPa
  • 298K (25°C)

Standard enthalpy change of combustion (∆cHƟ)

  • Definition: The enthalpy change when 1 mole of compound is formed from its elements in their standard states under standard conditions.
  • Example: 1.5H2(g) + 0.5N2(g)→NH3(g)

Standard enthalpy change of formation (∆fHƟ)

  • Definition: The enthalpy change when 1 mole of compound is burned completely in oxygen in their standard states under standard conditions.
  • Example: H2(g) + 0.5O2(g)→H2O(l)

Mean bond enthalpy

  • Definition: The mean energy needed to break a covalent bond measured across a range of molecules.
  • ∆H = (Total energy absorbed when breaking bonds) - (Total energy released when forming bonds)
Calorimetry

The heat change in a reaction is given by the equation

q = mc∆T

q = heat change in J
m = mass in g
c = specific heat capacity in J g-1 °C-1
∆T = change in temperature in °C

 

The molar enthalpy change is given by the equation

∆H = - (q/1000n)

∆H = enthalpy change of reaction in kJ mol-1
q = heat change in J
n = number of moles in mol

 

Sources of error

  1. Heat loss to the surroundings
  2. Assuming the solution has the same specific heat capacity as water
  3. Assuming the solution has the same density as water
  4. Incomplete combustion
  5. Evaporation of water

Methods to reduce error

  1. Insulation: add a lid and a polystyrene cup
  2. Record the temperature for a few minutes before starting the reaction to obtain an accurate initial temperature
  3. Extrapolate the line to estimate heat loss to the point when the reaction starts to obtain an accurate temperature change
  4. Stir the mixture to ensure uniform heat spread

Practice questions

  1. 0.550 g of propanone was burned in a calorimeter containing 80 g of water. The temperature rose by 47.3 ºC. Calculate ΔcH for propanone given the specific heat capacity of water is 4.18 J g-1 K-1 .

  2. 25.0 cm3 of 2.00 mol dm3 nitric acid was reacted with 25.0 cm3 of 2.00 mol dm3 potassium hydroxide is an insulated cup. The temperature rose from 20.2 ºC to 33.9 ºC. Calculate ΔH for the reaction given the specific heat capacity of water is 4.18 J g-1 K-1 .

Answers

  1. -1669 kJ mol-1

  2. -57.3 kJ mol-1
Hess's law - The enthalpy change of a reaction is independent to the route taken.
